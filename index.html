About me

Designer who
actually ships.

Hey, I'm Paige — a 24-year-old interface designer based in Auckland with a Bachelor of Design (Interface Design) from the University of Waikato. I have a habit of making things look significantly better than I found them.

Most recently I was a Marketing Coordinator at Hunza Outdoor Lighting where I rebuilt their website from scratch, fixed a database-level pricing bug with SQL, ran social media, and made sure everything from spec sheets to in-building signage felt polished.

I sit at the intersection of visual design and front-end development. Equally comfortable in Figma or a code editor. Every graphic, illustration, and image across my work is hand-drawn by me in Illustrator.

How I work

Figma → user flow mapping → wireframes → interactive prototype → handoff specs. I write HTML/CSS/JS when needed to close the loop between design and build.

Design approach

How I solve interface problems.

↓

Reduce steps before adding features

Every click is a decision. Fewer decisions means faster work. Applied in Automote: 5→2 steps.

⇢

Design for scanning, not reading

Users skim. Hierarchy does the heavy lifting. Applied in Hunza product pages.

◈

Systems over screens

Consistent patterns reduce cognitive load. Applied across all three case studies.
