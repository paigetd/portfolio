01 Overview

Two brands, one site,
a lot of problems to fix.

Hunza Lighting and LuxR are two distinct premium outdoor lighting brands that needed to be merged onto a single website without losing the identity of either. I was brought on as Marketing Coordinator and ended up leading the full digital overhaul — from UX strategy through to building and maintaining the site in WordPress with custom HTML, CSS, JS, and PHP.

This wasn't a greenfield project. The existing Hunza site had real structural problems, a broken product database, and three different user audiences all being served the same generic experience. Everything had to work properly, not just look good.

02 The Problem

A premium brand let down
by its own website.

Before touching design, I audited the existing site. Visual issues were obvious. But digging deeper revealed structural problems no amount of good design could paper over. The site was actively losing enquiries.

01

No audience distinction

Architects, homeowners, and trade distributors all landed on the same page. No differentiated path forward for anyone.

02

Visual design undercut the brand

Hunza products are precision-engineered and expensive. The website looked cheap. Every page was actively damaging trust.

03

Broken product database

SKU conflicts were causing products to display incorrect pricing — a serious problem where individual fixtures cost thousands of dollars.

04

Two brands, no unified home

LuxR existed as a separate site with minimal presence. Neither brand was benefiting from the relationship.

05 Technical Challenge

The problem design alone couldn't fix.

Midway through the project I discovered a significant number of products displaying incorrect prices. For a brand where individual fixtures can cost several thousand dollars, this wasn't a minor bug — it was a trust and revenue issue.

After investigating via phpMyAdmin, I identified duplicate and conflicting SKU entries causing the pricing logic to pull from wrong records. I wrote a targeted SQL query to identify all affected SKUs, corrected the database entries, and added validation to prevent the conflict recurring.

Problem: Duplicate SKU entries in the WooCommerce product database causing incorrect prices across a subset of products.

Fix: Cleaned duplicate entries, standardised SKU format, updated pricing logic to enforce SKU uniqueness. All prices validated correct post-fix.

fix_duplicate_skus.sql
-- Find all duplicate SKUs
SELECT post_id,
       meta_key,
       meta_value
FROM wp_postmeta
WHERE meta_key = '_sku'
GROUP BY meta_value
HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;

-- Validate fix post-cleanup
SELECT COUNT(*) as dupes
FROM wp_postmeta
WHERE meta_key = '_sku'
GROUP BY meta_value
HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;

07 Testing & Iteration

What changed after feedback.

Feedback — distributors

Product filtering wasn't granular enough

Trade distributors couldn't filter by the specifications they actually spec to: IP rating, beam angle, wattage. They were manually cross-referencing external documents.

What I changed

Added technical spec filtering to product listings

Extended the filter system to include technical attributes. Distributors could now filter by IP rating, wattage, and application type directly from the listing page.

Feedback — sales team

LuxR felt like an afterthought

Sales reps reported customers asking whether LuxR still existed. The merger had made it feel absorbed rather than featured.

What I changed

Gave LuxR dedicated landing pages

Created brand-specific section pages for LuxR within the unified structure — retaining shared navigation while giving LuxR its own visual identity and product pages.

Constraints

  • No dedicated dev resource — design and build were both my responsibility, scope had to be realistic
  • Existing WooCommerce data couldn't be fully restructured without breaking live integrations
  • Brand guidelines for both Hunza and LuxR were loose, requiring interpretation rather than strict application
  • The site had to remain live and functional throughout the redesign — no clean-slate opportunity
08 Outcomes

What it actually delivered.

Unified brand, stronger presence

Hunza and LuxR on one platform reduced confusion and improved perceived credibility. Sales reps reported fewer "how are the brands related" queries post-launch.

Reduced friction to enquiry

Audience-based navigation cut the path from landing to enquiry from 6 clicks to 3. Distributor feedback confirmed the trade section was significantly easier to navigate.

Pricing errors resolved

The SQL fix corrected pricing on all affected products. No further pricing discrepancies reported in the months following, removing a significant trust and revenue risk.

09 Reflection

What I took away.

Merging two brands isn't just a design challenge. It's a negotiation between identities, audiences, and business reality. Getting it right meant listening before designing.

This project taught me that the hardest problems aren't visual, they're structural. Before a single screen was designed, I needed to understand who was using the site, what they needed, and how two distinct brands could share space without one feeling secondary.

Working directly with distributors and sales reps changed design decisions I thought were settled. The gap between what stakeholders assume users want and what users actually need is always larger than expected.
